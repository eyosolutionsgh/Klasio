# Klasio — Features

School management software for African private schools, Ghana first, covering creche and nursery through senior high school. Your school runs on its own server, your records are never mixed with another school's, and the software keeps working when the internet does not.

## 1. Student records

- Full child record — names, gender, date of birth, photograph, admission number, class, medical notes and allergies
- Enrolment status: active, transferred, withdrawn, graduated, with dates and reasons
- **Unlimited guardians per child**, each with their relationship, primary-contact flag, permission to collect the child, and any custody restriction
- Brothers and sisters share one guardian record rather than three copies
- **Admission numbers in your school's own pattern**, issued in sequence; a withdrawn child's number is never handed to somebody else
- Documents against each child — birth certificate, immunisation card, previous reports, anything you keep
- **Required-document checklists per level**, showing at a glance what is still outstanding for each child
- **Your own fields** on the student record — church, bus stop, boarding house, whatever your school records
- Levels from creche through SHS, classes and streams within them
- Class promotion at year end, carrying unpaid fees forward
- Transfers, withdrawals and graduation; graduation asks you to confirm properly because it cannot be undone
- Alumni archive
- **Bring your existing register in from a spreadsheet** using a template you download
- Search, filter by class or status, sort, and export to Excel
- **Student ID cards with QR codes**, usable at the gate and, later, the canteen
- Several campuses under one school

## 2. Admissions

- **Public application form** — a parent who saw your flyer or received your link applies from their phone in about two minutes, with no account
- Applicant pipeline: enquiry, applied, assessed, offered, accepted, enrolled, declined, with counts at each stage
- Applicants kept separate from enrolled pupils, so an enquiry never distorts your enrolment figures or fee reports
- Accepting an applicant creates their student record and keeps the history of how they arrived
- Admission letters
- Documents attached to each applicant
- A school-unique reference for every applicant

## 4. Marks and terminal reports

- Marks entry by class and subject, designed for a teacher on a phone
- **You define what is marked** — class tests, assignments, projects, end-of-term papers, each with its own total
- An assessment can apply school-wide, to one subject everywhere, or to one subject in one level only
- **SBA weighted to 30 and exam to 70** in the GES pattern, adjustable — early years may run entirely without an exam
- Only work actually marked counts, so a missed assignment does not silently become a zero
- **Grading schemes** — GES grades, NaCCA proficiency bands, early-years observation scales, chosen per level
- **Class positions** on subject average, with ties sharing a position
- **GES terminal report cards**, printed faithfully on A4 with your crest, name, motto, address and telephone
- Modern report card template as an alternative
- **Broadsheets and full tabulation** per class
- Conduct, interest, teacher remark and head remark on every report
- Attendance figures carried onto the report
- **Remark banks** — build up reusable teacher, head, conduct and interest comments, offered by how well the child did
- Reports are generated, checked, then **published** — publishing is what releases them to parents
- Regenerating a published report asks you to confirm, because families may already have read it
- **Results notifications** telling parents their child's report is ready
- **Report cards delivered over WhatsApp**
- Print exactly as shown, download as PDF, or export a whole class to Excel, CSV or PDF
- **BECE aggregate projection and WASSCE readiness analysis**
- Works with no internet

## 7. Fees and billing

- Fee items per term, for the whole school or per level — tuition, PTA, exams, books, admission
- **Optional items** like transport and feeding billed only to the children who take them
- **Bill a whole term at once**
- Roll the fee structure forward to next term
- **Unpaid balances carried forward**, so what a family owes is always the true cumulative figure
- **Nothing in the fee record is ever quietly edited or deleted.** A mistake is corrected by a visible correction that cancels the original, and both remain — which is exactly what you need when a parent disputes a balance
- Receipts and invoices numbered in strict sequence; two clerks taking money at the same moment cannot claim the same receipt number
- **Cash payments at the office**, producing a printed receipt with your crest and the child's photograph
- **Bank deposits** submitted with a photograph of the slip, which **do not touch the child's balance until your bursar confirms them**; rejections carry a reason
- Full statement of account per child
- **Defaulter lists**
- Collection summary — billed, collected, outstanding, and the percentage collected
- **Scholarships** for named children, each carrying a written reason — a scholarship without a stated reason is a favour, not a policy
- **Sibling discounts applied automatically** from the second child onward
- Waivers and discounts, by percentage or fixed amount, school-wide or per level, for a set period if you wish
- **Payment plans** — a bill paid in agreed parts on agreed dates, recording when each falls due without changing what is owed
- **Automatic fee reminders** on the day and hour you choose, worded by you, in a gentle version and a firmer one
- **Which families are likely to fall behind**, flagged before they do
- **Accounting exports and a double-entry ledger**
- Financial reports in Excel and PDF
- Export to Excel, CSV or PDF for your accountant

## 8. Payments

- **Connect your own Hubtel or Paystack account** so money settles directly into your school's account — Klasio never sits between you and your money
- **Mobile money** — MTN, Telecel, AT Money — and **card payments**
- **Payment links** you send a parent, usable without signing in to anything
- Every bill carries its own payment reference, which is what makes it impossible for a payment to be recorded twice or applied to the wrong child
- Payments confirmed directly with the provider rather than taken on trust
- **Reconciliation** — import your provider's settlement file and Klasio matches it against what you received, allowing for their charges
- **Importing a file never moves money.** It shows what matched and lists anything that did not, with the reason in plain English, for someone to resolve
- Diaspora payments through Flutterwave
- **Fee payment and balance checks by USSD**, for parents on feature phones

## 9. Drop-off, pickup and child safety

Built for the school gate, on an ordinary Android phone, in bright sunlight, held in one hand — in the browser, with no app for your staff to install or keep updated.

- **Authorised collection list per child** — guardians plus delegates such as a driver, an aunt or a neighbour
- **Delegates expire by default**, because "just this term" is how these arrangements really work
- **Custody restrictions respected**: a restricted arrangement requires a member of staff to override it and state why; **a blocked arrangement cannot be overridden at all**
- **Morning drop-off check-in** and afternoon check-out recorded on the same gate device
- **QR code scanning** from the pickup card or student ID
- **PIN entry as a fallback** for anyone without a smartphone
- **Printed pickup cards** always carry the code in writing, so a parent without a smartphone is never a second-class case
- **The authorised person's photograph** shown to gate staff before the child is released
- A clear verdict: allowed, needs an override, or refused with the reason
- A child cannot be released twice in one day
- **Permanent record of every drop-off and release** — who, how they were identified, when, and which member of staff. The name is captured at the time, so the record still reads correctly years later even if that delegate has since been removed
- Where staff overrode the system, **the reason is recorded separately** from how the person was identified
- **Instant text to the parent** — "Ama was picked up by Kofi Mensah at 15:42"
- **Same-day dismissal change requests** — a parent tells you today that someone different is collecting, and your office approves or declines with a note
- **Car line management** — parents announce their arrival as they approach, and a queue display tells staff which child to bring out next
- **Emergency broadcast and lockdown alerts**
- **Dismissal analytics** — waiting times, and history per parent
- Works with no internet, and a child can never be recorded as leaving twice because of it

## 12. The WhatsApp assistant

**An automated assistant on your school's WhatsApp number, answering parents around the clock without a member of staff touching the phone.**

It is a bot, and it is doing a job your office is doing by hand today. Most of what parents ask a school is the same handful of questions — what do I owe, did the results come out, was my child marked absent — asked by different parents at different hours, including the hours nobody is at the desk. The assistant answers those instantly, from your live records, and escalates the rest to your staff.

**There is nothing for a parent to install.** Their phone already carries their bank, their network operator and their government, and a school app would be one more competing for space. It would also put a download between a parent and their child's fee balance — and the parents most likely to skip that download are the ones a fee reminder is aimed at. WhatsApp is already open on their phone.

### What the assistant handles on its own

| A parent asks | It answers |
| --- | --- |
| What do I owe? | The current balance, and a short statement of what was billed and paid |
| Did the results come out? | Their child's terminal report, once you have published it |
| Has my child been absent? | The attendance record for the term |
| My child is unwell today | Records the absence and tells the class teacher |
| My sister is collecting today | Logs a pickup change for your office to approve |
| What's happening this term? | Your published notices and calendar |
| I want to speak to someone | Hands the conversation to your staff, with what was already said |

### What this is worth to a school

- **Your office stops answering the same question fifty times a term.** Fee balance queries alone are most of the front desk's WhatsApp traffic in a fees week
- **Parents get an answer at 9pm on a Sunday**, which is when many of them actually think about school fees
- **A parent who knows their balance pays sooner.** The most common reason a bill goes unpaid another week is that nobody could tell the parent what it was
- **Every answer comes from your live records**, so the assistant is never working from a figure that changed this morning
- **A parent only ever gets their own children's information**, and the assistant confirms who it is talking to before it discusses a child

### The limits, stated plainly

- **It answers a set list of questions.** Anything outside that list goes to a person rather than being guessed at — and a parent can ask for a person at any point
- Understanding questions asked in a parent's own free-form words, rather than from the set list, is covered separately in section 21
- **Unprompted messages go by SMS**, not WhatsApp — fee reminders, absence alerts and results notifications reach every parent whether or not they use WhatsApp

## 13. Parent portal

For a parent who wants a bigger screen — an ordinary web page, not an app. It can be pinned to a home screen if they want it there, and nothing is downloaded either way.

Built for a cheap phone with patchy signal.

- **No password to remember.** A parent enters their phone number or email and receives a six-digit code — **by SMS or by email, their choice**, which matters because texts to some networks fail in ways neither you nor they can fix
- **Several children on one account**, each labelled with their class since two children in a family can share a name
- Fees outstanding, and a clear note when nothing is owed
- Attendance — present, late, absent, excused, with the percentage
- **Terminal reports, but only those you have published**, downloadable as PDF
- Payment history with downloadable receipts
- **Pay fees directly from the portal**
- **Request a dismissal change** for a given date
- Your notices, your calendar, and any learning materials you have published
- A parent only ever sees their own children

## 17. Staff, roles and payroll

- Staff accounts — who may sign in, and as what
- **Thirteen ready-made roles**: head teacher, assistant head, head of department, class teacher, subject teacher, exams officer, bursar, accounts clerk, registrar, front desk, school nurse, librarian, IT administrator
- **Create your own roles** and name them whatever your school calls them
- **Adjust one person** without changing the role everyone else shares
- **Nobody can hand out authority they do not hold themselves** — otherwise whoever manages staff could quietly make themselves a bursar
- **Separation of duties is real**: your head can see the fee position but cannot take money, set fees or grant discounts; an accounts clerk can record a payment but cannot confirm their own bank deposits; your IT administrator can run the system without seeing a child's record or a single cedi
- **Staff only see the buttons they can actually use**
- **The proprietor's account cannot be narrowed or locked out** by anyone, including by mistake
- Changing a password signs that person out on every device — what you want the day a laptop goes missing
- Repeated wrong passwords lock out the attempt
- Password reset by email link or SMS code, whichever suits the person
- **Payroll** with SSNIT tiers and GRA PAYE
- **Payslips**, and bank or mobile money payout files
- **Staff attendance and leave**

## 20. Working without internet

Poor connectivity is normal in many schools, so Klasio treats offline as ordinary rather than as a fault.

- **The daily register works with no connection**
- **Marks entry works with no connection**
- **The gate works with no connection**
- Everything saves on the device and sends itself the moment a connection returns
- **Money is deliberately excluded.** Payments and receipts are never held on a device, because a payment sent twice takes a parent's money twice. Those need a connection, and Klasio says so plainly rather than pretending
- **Work is never lost quietly.** If a teacher's session has expired, their register waits for them to sign in rather than vanishing. If something was genuinely refused, the teacher is told which entry so they can put it right — it does not fade away unread
- **An older entry never overwrites a newer correction**, so a register that syncs late does not undo a correction made in the meantime
- Klasio can be **pinned to a phone's home screen** so it opens like an app — without anything being downloaded or updated from an app store
